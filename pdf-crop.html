About & limitations

What this does: Replaces the page rectangle (MediaBox) on every page so the trimmed region is what every downstream tool sees — viewers, printers, and rasterizers like pdftoppm or Ghostscript. The original content stream stays intact, so quality is identical to the source and text remains selectable; the trimmed area is just no longer part of the visible page. Ideal for chopping margins off scanned books or letterboxed slides without re-rendering.

How margins work: Each margin is a number in PDF points (1 pt = 1/72 inch). They're applied uniformly to every page, measured from each visible edge — so a rotated page is cropped from its visual top/right/bottom/left, not from its raw PDF coordinates.

Privacy: Everything runs in your browser. The first run pulls ~2 MB of JS from cdn.jsdelivr.net for pdf.js and pdf-lib; both are cached afterward.

All data stays on your device.
